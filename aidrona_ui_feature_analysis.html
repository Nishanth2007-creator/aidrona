Blood crisis flow — 6 steps
1. Crisis trigger
User inputs blood type + GPS location + urgency via text or voice
2. AI triage
Gemini computes severity score 1–10; ≥8 = high-priority dispatch
3. Donor ranking
Top 3–5 donors by fitness score within 5 km radius get popup
4. Popup dispatch & acceptance
Blood type, distance, urgency shown. Accept locks in the donor. Decline routes to next.
5. Timer & escalation
No response → radius expands (5→15 km), blood banks queried in parallel
6. Doctor verification
Cleared: donation proceeds + score improves. Unfit: re-dispatch + permanent score penalty.
Vaccine / rabies flow — 5 steps
1. Incident report
Animal type, bite severity, time. AI assesses rabies risk level.
2. Hospital search
Map of nearby hospitals with real-time rabies vaccine stock counts. User picks, receives Dose 1.
3. Auto schedule creation
Full WHO PEP protocol generated on Dose 1 log — Day 0, 3, 7, 14, 28.
4. Pre-dose stock check
Before each due date, app checks chosen hospital stock. If empty, finds nearest alternative automatically.
5. Check-in & passive tracking
User confirms dose taken → marks schedule complete → decrements hospital stock by 1 automatically.

These are my design and feature recommendations on top of what both documents specify — things that will make AIdrona genuinely excellent.

1
Emergency-first visual design
Use a deep crimson + off-white palette. The emergency popup must be full-screen with massive tap targets — not a small notification card. In a crisis, users are panicking.
2
Radial fitness score gauge
Display the fitness score as an arc/radial gauge (0–100), not just a number. Users will understand their health standing visually. Color-code: green >70, amber 40–70, red <40.
3
Animated escalation radius on the map
Show a pulsing ring that visibly expands from 5→15 km on the requester's live map when the timer escalates. This reassures the requester the system is still working.
4
Voice input for crisis triggering
Someone in an emergency may not be able to type accurately. Add voice-to-text for the blood crisis form — blood type and urgency can be spoken in seconds.
5
Medical record expiry reminders
Records expire after 6 months. Show a countdown banner on the health dashboard 30 days before expiry — otherwise the donor silently becomes ineligible with no warning.
6
WhatsApp / SMS fallback alerts
Push notifications fail when the app is closed or data is weak. For India, sending a WhatsApp message or SMS as backup for the emergency popup is critical for rural users.
7
QR code for doctor sign-off
Hospitals can scan a donor's profile QR code on-site to instantly mark them Fit or Unfit — instead of requiring hospital staff to navigate a full dashboard under time pressure.
8
Tamil & Hindi localisation
This is built for India. All critical screens — especially the emergency popup and eligibility status — should support Tamil and Hindi. English-only will exclude a large portion of your real users.
9
Dose schedule calendar widget
The vaccine tracking screen should show a visual timeline, not a plain list. Show which doses are done (✓), upcoming (with days remaining), and missed — with a reschedule option for missed doses.
10
Progressive onboarding — medical data last
Don't ask for hemoglobin and prescriptions during signup. Let users enter and explore first, then prompt them to "complete your AIdrona profile" to unlock donor eligibility. Reduces drop-off.
